Reading it

The objective categories (Crypto, Weather, Tech, Geopolitics, Mentions) are well-calibrated in the newest data — as expected. The judgment categories shifted: Politics, which historically carried the bias, is now calibrated-to-slightly-reverse; only Finance still overprices longshots. Several categories (Sports ~0.90, Esports ~0.90) tip into a mild reverse, echoing the overall May–Jun slope of 0.949 from the time-trend analysis.

Caveats. The strongest reverse tilts sit in noisy one-event families (Iran 0.76, Economy 0.74 — small n, the Israel–Iran conflict cluster) — discount those. The robust movers are Politics, Sports, Esports (large n). The new V2 data validated clean on overall calibration (cal-err <0.01), so the mild reverse looks real (a mature-window favorite-lag effect) rather than a pipeline artifact — but it's worth a dedicated look. Categories are native-tag soft-vote primaries; Finance/Politics boundary cases are the workflow's known residual.

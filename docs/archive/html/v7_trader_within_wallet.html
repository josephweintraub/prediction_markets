Within a trader's own trajectory, FLB is flat at ~+0.02. Early, mid, and late thirds of a wallet's history all carry the same bias (+0.021 / +0.024 / +0.019, all significant). What this means — and the catch for ①

At the trader level, FLB is sticky: it does not wash out with individual experience. That's a clean, somewhat striking result on a within-person design.

Important honesty check on ① (series learning): the prior-settlements result I showed earlier was the bucketed / cross-sectional version, and it has the same composition trap as the raw ② — "many prior settlements" markets are the high-cadence recurring series (crypto/sports dailies), which are well-priced for category reasons, while "novel" series skew political. So that apparent "series learns" gradient may be category in disguise, exactly like ②'s raw gradient was. The clean ① is the within-series test — does a fixed series de-bias across its own successive instances (the market analog of what we just did for traders) — which I have not run yet.

So the honest scoreboard: trader-level learning → no (clean). Market/series-level learning → still open, pending the within-series version. If the within-series test also comes back flat, the story becomes "FLB is a stable property of market type (judgment vs objective), not something either traders or series learn away" — which would itself be a sharp, defensible finding.

Next: ① the within-series test (fixed series, early vs late instances) to mirror this within-wallet design — then ③ resolution-ambiguity and ④ within-event horse-race FLB.

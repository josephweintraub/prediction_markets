Is the newer data better calibrated?

Canonical FLB (D10−D1 spread + calibration slope, 3-way clustered), our standard filters — BUY-only, price 0.01–0.99, mature 25–80%, no bots, up/down excluded (via outcome∉{Up,Down} since eventSlug is sparse on new months). 37.6M qualifying trades.

Short answer: largely yes — but it's a category story, not a uniform shift. Overall calibration tightened from a mild FLB in 2025 (slope 1.039) to dead-on in early 2026 (slope 1.000), with a slight reverse tilt in the brand-new May–Jun V2 data (slope 0.949). By category, the objective / fast-resolving categories (Sports, Crypto, Tech, Weather, Iran) converged to well-calibrated, while the judgment categories — Politics and Finance — kept their FLB (slopes still 1.05 / 1.09).

Overall — calibration over time

The curve flattens and the slope walks toward 1.0 (and just past it) as we move to recent data. Read slope as the directional signal — the small negative D10−D1 spreads in recent buckets sit on slopes ≈1.0, i.e. well-calibrated boundary-decile wobble, not a strong reversal (same lesson as the crypto/sports check).

Watch the newest bucket: May–Jun 2026 (the new V2 extraction) shows a mild but significant reverse (spread −0.028, slope 0.949, t −2.7). It's small and likely composition (the new months are dominated by uncategorized high-frequency markets), but worth a closer look once those markets are categorized.

Across the board? For the objective categories, yes — Sports is the headline: from a strong −0.097 (slope 0.95) to +0.011 (slope 1.015); Crypto, Tech, Weather, Iran, Geopolitics all land near slope 1.0 recently. But Politics (+0.042, 1.051) and Finance (+0.051, 1.085) carry essentially the same FLB they always have — the genuine, judgment-driven bias doesn't wash out with time. Culture stays mildly biased too.

Takeaway

The market got better-calibrated where it can — objective, frequently-repeated, fast-resolving questions (sports, crypto, weather) are now priced almost exactly right, a real improvement over older data. The residual FLB lives where it always has: the judgment categories (Politics, Finance), which stay over-pricing longshots / under-pricing favorites regardless of period. So "newer = better calibrated" is true on average and dramatic for objective markets, but it's not uniform — and that non-uniformity is itself the FLB story (bias concentrated in hard-to-learn, judgment markets).

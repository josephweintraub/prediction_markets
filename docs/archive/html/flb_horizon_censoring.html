Resolution censoring: the mechanism, the measurement, and the corrected FLB story

Follow-up to the session audit. Three findings: (1) the trades dataset drops unresolved markets at build time; (2) the dropped mass is precisely the long-horizon judgment markets where FLB lives; (3) horizon-matched, the "newest data is calibrated" story survives only in weakened form — and the Politics/Finance headlines were composition artifacts.

1 · The mechanism — censoring is baked into the dataset, not the analysis

build_trades.py Stage 4 INNER JOINs mapped events to Gamma's closed=true markets. Any trade on a market without a Gamma resolution at build time (2026-06-24) never enters trades_clean. The dataset is therefore not "all trades through June 22" — it's "all trades on markets resolved by June 24." A rebuild later would contain more trades. Build log: Resolved: 1,048,419,713 / 1,086,023,186 mapped (96.5%).

Measured against the surviving raw on-chain events (86 GB of raw_events.parquet): ~0% of trade mass is censored through mid-2025, rising to 4.3% of events in May 2026 and 7.5% of events / 12.2% of dollars in June 2026. Lifetime total: 3.35% of events, 5.18% of dollars.

The dropped mass since May 2025 is $3.7B across ~20K markets — and it isn't random. It's the open long-horizon judgment markets:

Three clean results:

  1. Classic FLB is a long-horizon phenomenon. In 2025 it sits in 30–120d (1.057, t=+5.7) and >120d (1.062, t=+3.3) markets; everything under 30 days was already calibrated.
  2. The improvement into early 2026 is partly real — within the same long-horizon classes, Jan–Apr 2026 slopes drop to 1.017/1.027 and lose significance. That part is not composition.
  3. May–Jun cannot be evaluated where it matters: its >120d cell has n=34K (vs 3.6M in 2025) because those trades were censored out of the dataset. The mild reverse tilt in its short-horizon cells (0.93–0.97) is the only real signal, likely bot/flow-related (stale bot list caveat).

The two headlines from flb_newest_native.html die here: short-horizon Politics was never biased (1.026 n.s. in 2025 → 1.015 n.s. in May–Jun) — the "collapse from 1.049 to 0.894" compared long-horizon-inclusive 2025 Politics against short-horizon-only 2026 Politics. 5 · Related discovery: the native meta pull is closed-markets-only

native_repull.py fetches closed="true" on both the events and markets endpoints. "Will China invade Taiwan by end of 2026?" is not in native_market_meta.parquet — no open market is. The "99.98% coverage" figure was validated against the resolution-censored trades, so the gap was invisible (the same censoring hid it). Consequences: the tag taxonomy covers closed markets only (fine for FLB, which conditions on resolution; incomplete as a general market-dimension table), and any forward-looking / open-market analysis needs a supplementary pull.

6 · Fixes, ranked

  1. Re-frame, don't re-pull (now): all FLB results conditioned on resolution remain internally valid; the "newest data is calibrated across the board" claim becomes "short-horizon markets are calibrated (and were in 2025 too); long-horizon FLB declined through early 2026; May–Jun long-horizon is not yet gradeable."
  2. Resolutions refresh (cheap, periodic): re-fetch Gamma closed markets and re-run Stages 4+6 from the surviving raw_events.parquet — no Polygon/Alchemy cost. Each refresh converts newly-resolved markets' trades into the dataset; the May–Jun long-horizon cohort becomes analyzable in a few months.
  3. Fix the build for the future: change Stage 4's INNER JOIN to a LEFT JOIN (keep unresolved-market trades with NULL winner) so completeness stops depending on the build date and censoring becomes an explicit analysis-time filter. Design decision — changes dataset semantics/row counts.
  4. Complete the native meta: re-run native_repull.py without closed=true (or a second pass with closed=false) to add open markets.
  5. Chase the small leak: ~$120M of closed-by-Jun-21 markets missed by the Jun-24 build (sports matches) — check the Gamma resolutions parse for coverage of sports/negRisk events.

Verdict: our trades data validates. An independent extraction agrees with ours on 99.8% of tokens, 99.999% of resolutions (7 disagreements in 944,141), and 0.99 correlation in per-token fill counts and dollar volume — and the entire FLB-by-category result reproduces (spread correlation 0.837). The only real differences are recency (theirs is more up-to-date) and a tiny, uniform slope attenuation that follows from it.

Our 1.377B rows = 688.5M economic fills × 2 (a BUY row + a SELL row each); their 766M ≈ one row per fill. So at the fill level the two are within 11%. Token overlap: 1,498,581 shared, only-ours 2,681 (0.18%), only-theirs 120,869 (newer markets).

Resolution agreement

For every token cleanly resolved in both datasets, does "did this token win" match? 944,134 of 944,141 agree — 99.9993%. Just 7 disagree (UMA re-resolutions / dispute edge cases). Our outcomes — the thing FLB depends on most — are correct.

Per-token count ratio (theirs/ours): median 1.016, p25 1.000, p75 1.041, p95 1.150. 99.2% of tokens within 2×.

The one substantive difference is recency. Theirs syncs to mid-2026; ours stops at the Jan-2026 ingest. So their record runs longer (right side of the time chart) and carries ~8.5% more volume, concentrated in the high-frequency recent categories. Same markets, more of their tail — not a discrepancy in what we both cover.

The headline reproduces. Politics +0.0438 vs +0.0437 (essentially identical), Finance +0.051 vs +0.050, Geopolitics +0.051 vs +0.042 — all the judgment categories carry significant FLB on both. Sports/Crypto/Tech sit near zero (slopes ≈1.0) on both. Cross-dataset spread correlation is 0.837; signs agree in every category.
Caveats, both minor. (1) Their slopes run ~0.010 lower on average (attenuated toward 1.0) — the extra recent, well-priced crypto/weather/esports markets dilute the mix. (2) A few small-n categories (Iran, Esports, Crypto) wobble in magnitude but stay within noise and keep the same broad sign. Nothing moves the conclusion.

Bottom line

A fully independent re-extraction of Polymarket from the Polygon chain reproduces our dataset on coverage (99.8%), resolutions (99.999%), per-token activity (r=0.99), and the FLB-by-category result (r=0.837). Our trades data is correct. The differences are explainable and benign: theirs is newer (more recent fills, +8.5% volume) and our 2× row count is the buy+sell encoding, not duplication. Nothing here flags an error in our pipeline.
